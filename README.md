## 🎯 Objective

**MINDSTAT** explores how meditation practices influence **academic stress levels** among undergraduate students. Using quantitative and machine learning approaches, the project analyzes behavioral and emotional indicators to classify stress before and after meditation. The goal is to build predictive models that identify how meditation contributes to mental well-being and academic balance.  

---

## 🧩 Problem Statement  

Academic stress is one of the most significant mental health concerns faced by college students. Continuous exposure to academic pressure affects focus, mental stability, and performance.  
This project investigates whether **regular meditation practices can statistically and predictively reduce stress levels**, leveraging **machine learning** for analysis and interpretation.  

---

## 🧠 Project Workflow  

| Stage | Description |
|-------|--------------|
| **1️⃣ Data Collection** | Gathered responses and stress metrics from UG students through survey-based data (pre- and post-meditation). |
| **2️⃣ Data Cleaning & EDA** | Handled missing values, normalized variables, and visualized trends in stress distribution. |
| **3️⃣ Feature Engineering** | Extracted attributes like sleep duration, study hours, concentration levels, and meditation frequency. |
| **4️⃣ Model Training** | Trained multiple ML algorithms to classify and predict stress levels. |
| **5️⃣ Model Evaluation** | Compared performance metrics (accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score) to find the most reliable model. |
| **6️⃣ Interpretation** | Interpreted model insights to understand meditation’s measurable impact on academic stress. |

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  

**Key findings from EDA:**  
- 🧘 **Meditation frequency** and 😴 **sleep hours** show a strong negative correlation with stress.  
- Students practicing meditation for **>20 minutes daily** demonstrated **significantly reduced stress levels**.  
- **Female participants** reported higher baseline stress, but also greater improvement post-meditation.  
- Visualization revealed clear separation between **pre- and post-meditation** stress distributions.  

---

## 🧠 Insights  

| Category | Observation |
|-----------|-------------|
| 🕊️ **Mental Calmness** | Consistent meditators show improved attention and emotional stability. |
| 📚 **Academic Focus** | Meditation correlates with higher academic engagement and better stress management. |
| ⏰ **Sleep Quality** | Post-meditation sessions led to improved sleep hours in over 60% of participants. |
| 💬 **Student Feedback** | Most participants reported subjective relief and better concentration post-practice. |

---

## 🔍 Feature Importance  

The **Random Forest Classifier**, our best-performing model, highlighted the following as most influential features:  

- 🧘 **Meditation Duration (mins/day)**  
- 😴 **Average Sleep Hours**  
- 📈 **Academic Pressure Score**  
- 💭 **Mindfulness Score**  

These features collectively contributed to **over 75% of the model’s predictive power**.

## 🧩 Results  

| Observation | Inference |
|--------------|-----------|
| 📉 **Stress Drop** | Average stress level reduced by **32%** post-meditation sessions. |
| ⏱️ **Optimal Practice Time** | Students practicing **15–20 minutes daily** saw the largest improvement. |
| 📈 **Model Performance** | **Random Forest** achieved ~**94.7% accuracy**, outperforming other models. |
| 🧩 **Feature Effect** | **Meditation Duration** & **Sleep Hours** were most predictive of stress level. |

---

## 🧘 Conclusion  

* Meditation **significantly reduces academic stress levels**, validated both statistically and by machine learning models.  
* **Random Forest** achieved the best accuracy and interpretability (~94.7%).  
* **Meditation duration** and **sleep quality** strongly influence stress reduction outcomes.  
* The study demonstrates how **AI can quantify mental well-being** impacts in educational environments.  
* Integrating meditation into student routines can lead to measurable improvements in **focus**, **emotional control**, and **academic performance**.
